Anything Wok

Take a nice evening wok

This is a skill everyone should know: how to make a meal out of what you have. Basically, just grab anything that looks good in the fridge and throw it in a wok, add some sauce, and you are done. Serve it with rice, noodles, a salad, or as it is.

Pointer on what you could use:

  • Onions
  • Lettuce
  • Tomatoes
  • Cucumber
  • Left-over meat
  • The list goes on...
  1. Cut your vegetables and your meat into bite size pieces
  2. If you are using garlic (which you should), mince it or grate it
  3. Heat your wok until it's really hot
  4. Add you oil of choice, but don't use an oil with low smoke point like olive or sesame
  5. Throw in your aromatics, eg. garlic, green onion, onion, ginger, carrot, etc.
  6. Add your ingredients in the order of longest cooking time first. Raw meat comes before cooked, and so on
  7. Add some sauce. It could be soy sauce, oyster sauce, hoisin, ketchup, a mixture of all of the. Something that's deep in flavor. Don't add too much though, you're not looking for a stew

That's it, you've got dinner!
